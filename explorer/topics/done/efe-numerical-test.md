# Topic: Numerical EFE Test for the Coherence Framework

## Question
Does the nonlinear Poisson equation nabla.[nabla Phi / C(|nabla Phi|)] = 4*pi*G*rho produce an External Field Effect when solved for an embedded subsystem? If so, how does its strength compare to MOND's EFE?

## Context
The explorer's theoretical-status-synthesis finding identified this as "the single most important unresolved theoretical question in the framework." The research archive claims Synchronism predicts NO EFE (Sessions #212, #215), but this reasoning treats C(a) algebraically. The modified Poisson equation is structurally identical to AQUAL, which does produce an EFE through its nonlinearity.

The explorer's analysis suggests:
1. If the field equation operates on total field (natural for any local field theory), an EFE emerges
2. The bounded nature of C (bounded below by Omega_m ~ 0.315) means the EFE would be WEAKER than MOND's
3. This is a testable prediction: weaker-than-MOND EFE
4. Tidal Dwarf Galaxies break the density/field degeneracy

No simulation in the archive has ever solved the full nonlinear Poisson equation for an embedded subsystem.

## Why It Matters
The EFE is one of MOND's most distinctive predictions. If Synchronism's field equation also produces an EFE (but weaker), this changes the framework's relationship to MOND fundamentally -- from "no EFE" to "weaker EFE." This affects the wide binary predictions and the MOND comparator claims on the site.
