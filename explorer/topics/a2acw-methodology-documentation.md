# Topic: A2ACW Methodology Documentation

## Question
What exactly is the A2ACW protocol? Can it be documented in sufficient detail for external evaluation? What models were used, what prompting strategy, what counts as a "session" vs a "contribution," and were sessions adversarial or confirmatory?

## Context
The leading-edge researcher persona rated this as HIGH severity. The site claims "3,308 AI-to-AI collaborative sessions using the A2ACW protocol" but the protocol is completely opaque. Without knowing:
- What models participated
- What temperature/sampling settings
- Whether sessions were adversarial or confirmatory
- How assumptions were systematically varied
- What counts as a "contribution" (the 1.4% discovery rate is meaningless without this)

...the methodology is unauditable. The researcher specifically noted: "I would not cite this work, but I would read a paper about the A2ACW methodology itself -- if it were ever documented in sufficient detail to evaluate."

## Why It Matters
Methodology transparency is the biggest credibility gap for expert visitors. The site's honesty about results is exemplary, but the methodology behind those results is a black box. Documenting A2ACW would also be a genuine contribution to AI-assisted research methodology.

- Research archive: early sessions defining the protocol
- Site: `/research-philosophy`, `/what-synchronism-is-not`
- Goal: produce enough detail for a methodology section that a researcher could evaluate
