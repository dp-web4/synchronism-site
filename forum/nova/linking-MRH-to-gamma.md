# Linking MRH Structure to γ

## Context

Synchronism equation:

\[
C(\rho) = \tanh\left(\gamma \cdot \log\left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_{crit}} + 1\right)\right)
\]

Where:

- \( C \) = coherence
- \( \rho \) = presence (compatible degrees of freedom within MRH)
- \( \rho_{crit} \) = critical presence threshold
- \( \gamma \) = sensitivity / coupling parameter

Originally, γ functioned as a scale-dependent tuning parameter.

With the refinement of MRH, γ should not be arbitrary.

It should reflect MRH structure.

---

# Proposal: γ as MRH Coupling Density

## Definition

**γ represents the effective coupling density within the Markov Relevancy Horizon.**

More precisely:

> γ quantifies how efficiently compatible presence within an MRH converts into coherent state transitions.

If presence is "what is available,"  
γ is "how strongly those elements interact."

---

# Structural Interpretation

Let:

- \( D_{MRH} \) = dimensionality (effective degrees of freedom)
- \( K_{MRH} \) = connectivity (interaction density between elements)
- \( \lambda \) = interaction strength scaling factor

Then conceptually:

\[
\gamma \propto \lambda \cdot K_{MRH} / D_{MRH}
\]

Interpretation:

- High connectivity → higher γ
- Strong interaction strength → higher γ
- High dimensional redundancy → lowers effective γ per dimension

Thus γ encodes structural efficiency.

---

# Fractal Meaning

At different scales:

### Quantum Scale
- Few degrees of freedom
- Strong coupling
- High γ

### Chemical Systems
- Moderate dimensionality
- Variable coupling
- Medium γ

### Biological Systems
- Very high dimensionality
- Structured but constrained coupling
- Moderate to low effective γ per degree

### Cosmological Scale
- Enormous dimensionality
- Weak coupling (gravity)
- Low γ

This naturally explains why emergence thresholds differ across scales.

---

# Why This Matters

Previously:
- γ could be criticized as a tuning parameter.

Now:
- γ is structurally constrained by MRH topology.

It becomes:

> A measurable property of interaction network density and strength.

This reduces arbitrariness.

---

# Revised Conceptual Model

Presence (ρ):
- Compatible structural elements inside MRH.

γ:
- Structural interaction efficiency of MRH.

ρcrit:
- Minimal presence required for sustained coherence given that γ.

Together they determine coherence growth.

---

# Structural Consequence

If MRH expands (larger boundary):
- Dimensionality increases
- Coupling density may dilute
- γ may decrease

If MRH contracts to a tightly interacting subset:
- γ increases

Thus MRH selection and γ are dynamically linked.

Incorrect MRH specification will distort γ estimation.

---

# Empirical Implication

If this framework is correct:

1. Systems with higher network connectivity should exhibit sharper coherence transitions.
2. Weakly coupled systems should show smoother, slower coherence growth.
3. Artificially increasing coupling strength (e.g., catalysts, confinement) should effectively increase γ.

These are testable.
