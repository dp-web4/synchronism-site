#### CLAIM 1: Shared-Environment Decoherence Protection (QUANTUM)

**The claim**: Entangled pairs in the SAME noise environment decohere slower than pairs in independent environments. Decoherence rate: Γ = γ²(1 - c), where c is environmental noise correlation.

**Why it's new**: Standard QM models decoherence as local — each qubit decoheres independently. Synchronism says entanglement is ONE extended phase pattern, so correlated noise (same bath) preserves it. This is not an interpretation difference — it predicts different T₂ times.

**Status**: **CONFIRMED by literature** (PRL 2024: 10x improvement with c=0.90; arXiv 2405.14685: shared bath reduces dephasing). The formula quantitatively matches.

**Where it is on the site**: NOWHERE. The `/quantum-predictions/` page lists 6 protocols from Sessions #368-370 but none mention this result. The `/decoherence-mrh/` page discusses decoherence conceptually but doesn't present Γ = γ²(1-c).

**Impact if promoted**: This is the single strongest result in the entire framework — a quantitative prediction, derived before the confirming experiments, with published literature support. It should be front and center.

#### CLAIM 2: Bell Violation Temporal Decay (QUANTUM)

**The claim**: CHSH Bell violation decays exponentially with time: |S(t)| = S_max × e^{-Γt}, where Γ = γ²(1-c). At distances d = n·λ₀, correlation revives (Bell nonlocality freezing/revival).

**Why it's new**: Standard QM predicts no fundamental distance dependence for entanglement quality. Synchronism predicts an oscillatory pattern c(d) = cos²(πd/λ₀) tied to environmental bath wavelength.

**Status**: **CONFIRMED by literature** — arXiv 2508.07046: "Geometry-Controlled Freezing and Revival of Bell Nonlocality" matches the model directly. Bell revival at distance nodes confirmed by multiple sources.

**Where it is on the site**: NOWHERE. Not on any page.

**Impact if promoted**: A second confirmed prediction from the same framework, independent of Claim 1. Two confirmed predictions from one equation (Γ = γ²(1-c)) is significant.

#### CLAIM 4: Consciousness Formula C = f(γ, D, S) and Free Will as Constrained Indeterminacy (CONSCIOUSNESS)

**The claim**: Consciousness requires three conditions simultaneously: γ in the right range (coherence), D sufficiently high (dimensional embedding / representational richness), and S sufficiently high (self-modeling depth). Free will = the agent's coherence pattern influencing its own evolution at the γ ≈ 1 boundary, where multiple futures are genuinely accessible.

**Why it's new**: Other theories either avoid quantification entirely (philosophy of mind) or propose single measures (IIT's Φ). The three-parameter model makes specific predictions: a system can fail consciousness by lacking any ONE of γ, D, or S — explaining why thermostats (low D), random number generators (low S), and decoherent systems (wrong γ) aren't conscious despite meeting some criteria.

**Status**: Untested. The free will formulation generates one distinctive prediction: decision-making should show signatures of γ ≈ 1 boundary dynamics (neither fully random nor fully determined). But the current site page (`/free-will/`) doesn't state this as a testable claim.

**Where it is on the site**: `/hard-problem/` presents C = f(γ, D, S) well. `/free-will/` is **the weakest page in the consciousness cluster** — no equation, no link to C formula, and the Status section actively disclaims: "doesn't generate testable predictions distinct from standard neuroscience."

#### CLAIM 5: Dark Matter as Incomplete Decoherence (COSMOLOGY)

**The claim**: Dark matter effects are regions where quantum-to-classical decoherence is incomplete — not a new particle, not modified gravity, but a failure of the classical limit. The coherence function C(a) modifies effective gravity because the system hasn't fully decohered at low accelerations.

**Why it's new**: MOND modifies gravity empirically. ΛCDM adds a particle. Neither explains WHY the anomaly appears at a specific acceleration scale. Synchronism derives a₀ = cH₀/(2π) from the coherence transition — the scale where decoherence becomes incomplete IS the MOND scale.

**Status**: Consistent with data (14,760 galaxies, a₀ within 10%). Listed on publication-roadmap at 0.88 readiness, 40% circularity.

**Where it is on the site**: Mentioned on `/publication-roadmap/` as a manuscript title. NOT presented on any conceptual page. The `/why-synchronism/` page describes galaxy success but doesn't frame it as "dark matter = incomplete decoherence."

**Impact if promoted**: This is the most provocative cosmological claim. Even if the quantitative prediction is MOND-equivalent, the mechanism is novel.
