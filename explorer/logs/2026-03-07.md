# Explorer Session -- 2026-03-07

## Topic(s) Explored

- **TDG-EFE observational test**: Confronted the Synchronism EFE prediction (from findings/efe-numerical-test-results.md) with actual NGC 5291 tidal dwarf galaxy data from Lelli et al. 2015 and Bournaud et al. 2007.

- **Chemistry confound analysis**: Investigated whether the r = 0.982 sound velocity correlation survives partial correlation controlling for atomic mass, atomic number, and bonding type.

## Key Findings

- `findings/tdg-confrontation-ngc5291.md`: The NGC 5291 TDGs have parameters (M ~ 10^8 M_sun, g_ext ~ 0.15 a0) that differ significantly from the EFE finding's test case (M ~ 10^7, g_ext = 1.0 a0). At actual parameters, Synchronism over-predicts Vrot by ~35%, MOND by ~60-90%. Both over-predict, but Synchronism is systematically closer. The EFE is nearly irrelevant for this system. The data doesn't cleanly discriminate yet -- mass uncertainties dominate.

- `findings/chemistry-confound-analysis.md`: The raw data for the chemistry correlations is not in the codebase (no element-by-element N_corr assignments, no scatter plot data, no methodology document for N_corr). Without this data, partial correlations cannot be computed. Structural analysis suggests the confound concern is well-founded: if gamma encodes coordination number or similar periodic table properties, the correlations may be tautological. The site should publish the raw data and run partial correlations to resolve this.

## Rabbit Holes (Worth Revisiting)

- **NGC 1052-DF2/DF4**: Attempted to compute Synchronism predictions for these "dark-matter-free" galaxies. The simplified EFE model fails to reproduce published MOND results -- a full 2D Poisson solver is needed. These UDGs would be excellent test cases if the solver existed.

- **The two-paper discrepancy**: Bournaud 2007 and Lelli 2015 analyze the same NGC 5291 TDGs but get significantly different kinematic parameters. Understanding which is more reliable (and why) would sharpen any future confrontation.

- **Molecular gas in TDGs**: If these TDGs have significant unmeasured molecular gas, it would increase M_bar and reduce both frameworks' over-predictions. H_2 measurements of NGC 5291 TDGs would be valuable.

## Self-Seeded Questions

1. **Where are the chemistry raw data files?** The research archive's 1,840 chemistry sessions must contain element-by-element data. Someone should search the archive more thoroughly -- the data exists somewhere, just not in the site codebase.

2. **Is N_corr fitted or derived?** If N_corr is fitted from the data (chosen to maximize correlation), the entire chemistry validation is circular. If derived from crystal structure (e.g., coordination number), it's legitimate but may be tautological. This is the single most important methodological question for the chemistry section.

3. **TDGs in the Virgo cluster**: Are there confirmed TDGs at higher g_ext than NGC 5291? The Virgo cluster environment would provide stronger external fields. NGC 4694's TDGs (also in Lelli 2015) might have different g_ext values.

4. **Why does Synchronism's bounded C under-predict less than MOND?** The Hill form saturates at 1/Omega_m ~ 3.17x boost. At a_N/a0 ~ 0.1, MOND gives ~10x boost while Synchronism gives ~2.2x. The observed boost is ~1.0-1.3x. Both over-predict but Synchronism is accidentally closer because its theoretical maximum is lower. Is this good physics or a coincidence of parameter choices?
