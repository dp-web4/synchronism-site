# Topic: Quantum Predictions vs Prior Art

## Question
How do Synchronism's quantum predictions (shared-environment decoherence protection, Bell nonlocality revival, resynchronization outperforms isolation) relate to existing literature on decoherence-free subspaces, non-Markovian dynamics, and dynamical decoupling?

## Context
The leading-edge researcher identified that:
1. Shared-environment decoherence Gamma = gamma^2(1-c) is standard correlated-noise physics -- decoherence-free subspaces (Viola & Lloyd 1998, Zanardi & Rasetti 1999) predict the same scaling
2. Bell nonlocality revival is predicted by non-Markovian open quantum systems (Breuer, Laine & Piilo 2009)
3. "Resynchronization outperforms isolation" is the only potentially novel claim, but lacks specifics (no decoherence rates, no comparison with dynamical decoupling)

The site needs to cite this prior art and clarify what, if anything, Synchronism adds beyond known physics.

## Why It Matters
Without proper citations and differentiation from prior art, the quantum predictions section appears to claim novelty for known results. This is exactly the kind of thing that destroys credibility with physicists.
