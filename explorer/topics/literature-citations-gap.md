# Topic: Literature Engagement — The Biggest Credibility Gap

## Question
How should the site engage with the existing published literature? Which pages need citations most urgently, and what specific papers should be cited?

## Context
The 2026-02-28 four-persona visitor review identified "no literature citations with DOIs" as the **single biggest credibility gap** for scientific audiences. The researcher persona rated this as critical. No published paper is cited anywhere on the site by DOI, author, or title.

Specific gaps flagged:
- **MOND/a₀**: No citation of Milgrom 1983, Milgrom 1999, Verlinde 2017 who discuss the same cH₀ ~ a₀ coincidence
- **Born rule**: No citation of Zurek's envariance/quantum Darwinism (2003, 2005, 2024)
- **BAO predictions**: No engagement with DESI Year 1 (2024) or eBOSS analyses
- **Wide binary test**: No citation of Chae 2023, Hernandez 2023, Banik et al. 2024, Pittordis & Sutherland 2023
- **PRL 2024 decoherence**: Cited without DOI, authors, or title — unverifiable
- **CRT temporal scanning**: No comparison to 't Hooft's deterministic QM (2016)
- **Consciousness**: No comparison to IIT's Φ measurement protocol (Tononi et al.)

## Why It Matters
- A framework that doesn't know what's already been done can't establish what's new
- The grad student and researcher personas both independently flagged this
- Without citations, the site looks like it exists in a vacuum — which undermines the genuine honesty elsewhere
- Some of the site's claims may already be tested or constrained by existing data (e.g., BAO density dependence in DESI)

## Suggested Starting Points
- Prioritize the theory pages: /mond-unification, /born-rule, /key-claims, /top-5-tests
- Research repo: check if citations exist in session docs that didn't make it to the site
- For each claim page, ask: "What prior work makes the same or related claim? What existing data constrains this prediction?"
- Consider a references component that could be reused across pages
