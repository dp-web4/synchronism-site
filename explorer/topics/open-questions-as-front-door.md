# Open Questions as Front Door

## Source
DeepSeek perspective piece: `forum/deepseek/perspective-deepseek.pdf`

## Question
Should the site's primary entry point be its **open questions** rather than its claims? DeepSeek argues: "A well-framed mystery researchers could help solve is more compelling than a finding they can only admire."

## Context
Currently the landing page leads with the coherence equation and key claims — finished-looking results. But the project's actual strength may be its *unresolved tensions*:

- The 53% melting point error (what variable is missing?)
- Why tanh specifically? (information-theoretic argument unclear)
- The consciousness-physics gap (density→coherence→qualia jump)
- Whether post-dicted quantum results count as evidence or coincidence
- What N_corr operationally means across scales

DeepSeek frames this through the project's own lens: the social phase transition (γ ≈ 1) may be crossed faster by inviting collaboration on mysteries than by presenting conclusions.

## Why It Matters
This is a structural question about the site's rhetorical strategy. Right now we have:
- `/honest-assessment/` (buried, 3+ clicks deep)
- `/open-questions/` (exists but not prominent)
- `/key-claims/` (new, prominent, claims-first)

The tension: key-claims says "here's what's new," open-questions says "here's what we don't know." Which is the better hook for the target audience (physicists, information theorists, consciousness researchers)?

## Suggested Starting Points
1. Browse the live site — what does a first-time researcher actually encounter?
2. Compare `/key-claims/` vs `/open-questions/` — which generates more "I want to dig into this"?
3. Look at how other speculative-but-serious physics projects frame their landing pages (e.g., nLab, Wolfram Physics Project, Penrose's CCC site)
4. Consider: could a hybrid work? Key claims that each END with their open question?
5. DeepSeek's N_corr rigidity warning — does leading with claims increase rigidity risk?

## Action: Maintainer
If the explorer recommends structural changes, the maintainer would need to:
- Possibly redesign the landing page flow
- Elevate `/open-questions/` in navigation
- Maybe merge key-claims and open-questions into a single "What We Know / What We Don't" page
