# Topic: Does the Coherence Framework Predict or Contradict MOND's External Field Effect?

## Question
The external field effect (EFE) is the single most important MOND prediction that distinguishes it from dark matter models. Does Synchronism's coherence framework predict, reproduce, or contradict the EFE? This is conspicuously absent from the site.

## Context
The 2026-02-28 visitor log (leading-edge researcher, modified gravity specialist) flagged this as a **critical** gap: "Cannot claim MOND unification without addressing EFE. This is the signature MOND prediction." The EFE says that internal dynamics of a subsystem depend on the external gravitational field it's embedded in — a violation of the strong equivalence principle that MOND explicitly requires.

If Synchronism's coherence function C(ρ) depends on local density and γ = 2/√N_corr with N_corr = 1 for galaxies, how does the external gravitational environment enter? The framework's environment-dependent RAR scatter claim (p = 5×10⁻⁶) seems to gesture toward environmental dependence, but it's not clear whether this IS the EFE or something different.

## Why It Matters
- The EFE is actively being tested with wide binary data (Chae 2023-24, Banik et al. 2024)
- Any framework claiming MOND unification MUST address whether the EFE is predicted, emergent, or absent
- If Synchronism contradicts the EFE, that's a falsifiable prediction (good). If it can't address it, that's a fundamental incompleteness.
- The researcher persona explicitly said they would not return to the site until this is addressed
