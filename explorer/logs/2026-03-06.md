## Rabbit Holes (Worth Revisiting)

- **The two-C problem**: The site uses C_tanh (unbounded boost) while the research archive's galaxy fits use C_hill (bounded, Omega_m floor). These make fundamentally different predictions — especially for the EFE. The tanh form gives a STRONGER-than-MOND EFE; the Hill form gives a weaker one. This isn't cosmetic — the framework needs to commit.

- **Full 2D axisymmetric PDE solver**: The perturbative results are exact for g_int << g_ext but a full nonlinear solver would cover arbitrary field ratios. Worth building if the framework wants to make precise TDG predictions.

- **Quantum prediction timeline**: The key claims finding identified two "confirmed" predictions (Novel-8, Novel-9). Their credibility depends entirely on whether the derivation predated the experiments. Session dates vs publication dates need verification.

- **Lagrangian for the flux-form equation**: Writing out the action and deriving conserved quantities would establish the field-theoretic consistency. This is standard AQUAL territory but hasn't been done for the Synchronism interpolating function.
